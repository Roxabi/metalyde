Phase 2 — Customer Personas & Voice

Metalyde — Who We Are Building For

Four personas across the agency operating surface. Primary target: the solo/founding operator of a 2–10 person AI-augmented agency who sits at every broken seam simultaneously.

Signal sources: metalyde-x-signals.md + metalyde-web-signals.md (2026-06-16) Verbatim quotes attributed by @handle + date JTBD: Functional / Emotional / Social per persona
Priority Order
Persona Priority with Rationale

Prioritized by: density of signal volume, buyer control (pays + decides), proximity to all four broken seams, and addressable TAM within Lean Agency 2.0.

P1 — Primary Buyer

The Founder-Operator

2–8 person agency. Wears every hat. Controls the credit card. Feels every broken seam personally. Highest signal density in research. The person Metalyde must win.

P2 — Expansion Buyer

The Ops / Traffic Manager

First ops hire in 5–15 person agency. Decides or strongly influences tool stack. Exposed to all four seams as the human glue. High influence, lower direct authority.

P3 — Champion User

The Account / Project Lead

Day-to-day Metalyde user; surfaces scope creep and client friction. Not the buyer, but kills adoption if tool doesn't fit. Referral engine once converted.

P4 — Stakeholder User

The Creative / Delivery Lead

Cares about brief quality and handoff clarity, not billing. Key user for the brief→production seam. Adoption blocker if tool adds overhead; accelerator if it reduces brief ambiguity.

Why P1 Founder-Operator First
Signal Density

X research yields 8 of 12 top quotes from founder-operators venting directly. They are the most vocal, most searchable, most reachable through content.

Seam Exposure

At 2–8 people, the founder touches ALL FOUR seams: writes the brief, manages production, does billing, and delivers the report. No other persona has this exposure.

AI-Native Identity Pull

The "labor → leverage" identity shift (@Jacobsklug, @MLewisson10) targets exactly this persona. They are not just buying a tool — they are buying an identity upgrade.

Pain Points
  • Scope creep bleeds revenue silently — 57% of agency owners lose $1–5K/mo to unbilled extras (Ignition 2025)
  • Every broken seam requires context-switching to a different tool — briefs in Notion, tasks in Asana, billing in QuickBooks, reports in Google Slides

  • Client reporting is a 4-hour manual assembly job, every single month
  • "Golden handcuffs" — 1–2K retainers that don't cover the actual cognitive load

  • AI tools are fragmented and not connected to actual client work

  • Hire the right people, not hire out of desperation because ops are failing

Open Questions
Convergence Gate — Founder Must Resolve

These questions block spec development. Answers determine ICP targeting, pricing model, and go-to-market sequence.

1
Is the Founder-Operator the only ICP for launch, or do you sell to 5-15 person agencies where P2 (Ops Manager) holds budget?
These are different GTM motions: P1 = self-serve PLG (solo sign-up, credit card immediate); P2 = sales-assisted (demo, stakeholder buy-in, IT approval). Both valid but require different onboarding and pricing architecture.
2
Which broken seam is the entry point — billing (highest P1/P3 pain) or brief-to-production (highest P4 pain, AI showcase)?
Starting with billing captures the P1 "stop the revenue bleed" urgency and drives faster conversion. Starting with brief-to-production showcases the AI-native identity angle and creates stickier daily use. These lead to different MVP feature priorities.
3
Does Metalyde compete on the GoHighLevel migration path, or does it explicitly NOT target GHL users?
GHL has the largest installed base of SMB marketing agencies. Post-Bonsai/Zoom acquisition, there is a churn window. But GHL users have fundamentally different workflows (funnel-centric, not project-centric). Supporting migration doubles onboarding complexity.
4
What is the minimum client-facing surface at launch — reporting dashboard only, or a full client portal with brief approval and live delivery tracking?
A read-only reporting dashboard ships faster and eliminates the "clients won't log into a portal" objection entirely. A full portal creates stronger retention but adds auth, permissions, and UX complexity that could delay launch 6–10 weeks.
5
Is the "AI-native" identity angle a primary message (change who you are) or a feature message (automate the report)?
Identity-first messaging (@Jacobsklug, @MLewisson10 thesis) targets the aspirational P1 who wants to become an AI leverage business — higher willingness to pay, longer retention. Feature-first messaging ("generate your monthly report in one click") converts faster, targets broader P1–P3 audience, lower risk of overpromise.
